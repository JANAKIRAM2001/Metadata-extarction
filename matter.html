Landsat Metadata Explained

Collection Number

The Collection Number represents the version or edition of the Landsat data collection. It helps users track and identify different versions of Landsat data products.

Nadir or OffNadir

Nadir refers to the position of the satellite directly overhead the point on Earth's surface it is observing. OffNadir indicates that the satellite is positioned off to the side from directly overhead, resulting in images with different perspectives and potential distortions.

Station ID

The Station ID refers to the ground station or receiving station that receives the satellite data. These stations are strategically located around the world to ensure continuous communication with satellites as they orbit the Earth.

Ground Control Points Model

The Ground Control Points (GCPs) model refers to a set of known points on the Earth's surface with accurately measured geographic coordinates. These points are used to geometrically correct satellite imagery and ensure its accuracy and alignment with real-world locations.

Ground Control Points Version

The Ground Control Points Version indicates the version or iteration of the Ground Control Points model used for geometric correction of satellite imagery. It helps ensure the accuracy of the correction process and the alignment of satellite data with real-world locations.

Geometric RSME Model

The Geometric RSME Model represents the Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) associated with the geometric correction process of satellite imagery. It quantifies the average discrepancy between the corrected image coordinates and their corresponding ground control points on the Earth's surface. A lower RMSE value indicates a higher level of geometric accuracy in the corrected imagery.

Image Quality OLI

The Image Quality OLI represents the quality assessment score for the Operational Land Imager (OLI) sensor data. It evaluates various factors such as sensor performance, calibration, and data processing to provide a measure of the overall quality of the OLI imagery. A higher score generally indicates better image quality.

Image Quality TIRS

The Image Quality TIRS represents the quality assessment score for the Thermal Infrared Sensor (TIRS) data. Similar to Image Quality OLI, it evaluates the quality of TIRS sensor data, including factors such as sensor performance, calibration, and data processing. A higher score typically indicates better quality TIRS imagery.

Processing Software Version

The Processing Software Version indicates the version of the software used to process and generate the satellite imagery data. It identifies the specific software tools and algorithms applied during the data processing stage, which can impact the accuracy and quality of the resulting imagery.

Grid CellSize Panchromatic

The Grid CellSize Panchromatic refers to the size of the grid cells used in the panchromatic band of the satellite imagery. It represents the spatial resolution of the panchromatic band, with smaller cell sizes indicating higher resolution and finer detail in the imagery.

Geometric RMSE Model

The Geometric RMSE Model represents the Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) associated with the geometric correction process of satellite imagery. It quantifies the average discrepancy between the corrected image coordinates and their corresponding ground control points on the Earth's surface. A lower RMSE value indicates a higher level of geometric accuracy in the corrected imagery.

Geometric RMSE Model X

The Geometric RMSE Model X represents the Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) in the horizontal (X) direction associated with the geometric correction process of satellite imagery. It measures the accuracy of the horizontal alignment of the corrected image coordinates with respect to the ground control points on the Earth's surface.

Geometric RMSE Model Y

The Geometric RMSE Model Y represents the Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) in the vertical (Y) direction associated with the geometric correction process of satellite imagery. It measures the accuracy of the vertical alignment of the corrected image coordinates with respect to the ground control points on the Earth's surface.

Grid Cell Size Reflective

The Grid Cell Size Reflective represents the size of the grid cells used in the reflective bands of the satellite imagery. It defines the spatial resolution of the reflective bands, with smaller cell sizes indicating higher resolution and finer detail in the imagery.

Grid Cell Size Thermal

The Grid Cell Size Thermal represents the size of the grid cells used in the thermal band of the satellite imagery. Similar to the reflective bands, it defines the spatial resolution of the thermal band, with smaller cell sizes indicating higher resolution and finer detail in the imagery.

Reflective Lines

The Reflective Lines refer to the number of lines (rows) in the reflective bands of the satellite imagery. It specifies the total number of scan lines captured in the reflective bands during image acquisition.

Reflective Samples

The Reflective Samples refer to the number of samples (columns) in the reflective bands of the satellite imagery. It specifies the total number of pixel samples captured in each line of the reflective bands during image acquisition.

Thermal Lines

The Thermal Lines refer to the number of lines (rows) in the thermal band of the satellite imagery. It specifies the total number of scan lines captured in the thermal band during image acquisition.

Thermal Samples

The Thermal Samples refer to the number of samples (columns) in the thermal band of the satellite imagery. It specifies the total number of pixel samples captured in each line of the thermal band during image acquisition.

Ellipsoid

The Ellipsoid represents the mathematical model used to approximate the shape of the Earth's surface. It defines the geometric properties of the Earth's ellipsoidal shape and is commonly used in geospatial calculations and map projections.

FileName BFP OLI

The FileName BFP OLI is the filename associated with the Browse File Product (BFP) of the Operational Land Imager (OLI) data. It typically contains information about the acquisition date and time, as well as other metadata related to the OLI imagery.

FileName BPF TIRS

The FileName BPF TIRS is the filename associated with the Browse File Product (BPF) of the Thermal Infrared Sensor (TIRS) data. Similar to the OLI BFP filename, it contains information about the acquisition date and time, as well as other metadata related to the TIRS imagery.

FileName RLUT

The FileName RLUT is the filename associated with the Radiometric Look-Up Table (RLUT) file. This file contains calibration information used to convert digital numbers (DN) in the satellite imagery to radiance values, allowing for accurate analysis of the observed radiation.

Spacecraft ID

The Spacecraft ID indicates the name or identifier of the satellite used to capture the imagery. In this case, "LANDSAT_8" refers to the Landsat 8 satellite, which is part of the Landsat program operated by NASA and the USGS.

Sun Azimuth

The Sun Azimuth represents the azimuth angle of the sun, which is the angle between the direction of the sun and the north direction, measured clockwise in degrees from the north.

Map Projection

The Map Projection specifies the mathematical model used to represent the curved surface of the Earth on a flat map. In this case, "UTM" stands for Universal Transverse Mercator projection, which divides the Earth into zones and uses a transverse Mercator projection for each zone.

Acquisition Date

The Acquisition Date indicates the date when the satellite imagery was acquired. In this example, the date is "2024-02-11", which represents February 11, 2024.

Projection Attribute

The Projection Attribute provides additional parameters related to the map projection used for the imagery. It includes details such as the projection type, datum, zone, grid cell size, and orientation.

Cloud Cover

The Cloud Cover represents the percentage of the image area covered by clouds or cloud shadows. It provides an indication of the atmospheric conditions during image acquisition, with lower values indicating clearer skies.

Sensor ID

The Sensor ID indicates the specific sensor or instrument onboard the satellite used to capture the imagery. In this case, "OLI_TIRS" refers to the Operational Land Imager (OLI) and Thermal Infrared Sensor (TIRS) combination used in Landsat 8.

Roll Angle

The Roll Angle represents the angle of rotation of the satellite around its longitudinal axis. It is measured in degrees and can affect the orientation of the captured imagery.

Sun Elevation

The Sun Elevation represents the angle between the direction of the sun and the horizontal plane at the observation point. It indicates the height of the sun above the horizon and affects the illumination of the Earth's surface in the imagery.

Path

The Path identifies the specific orbital path followed by the satellite during image acquisition. It, along with the Row, helps to specify the geographic location of the imagery.

Row

The Row identifies the specific orbital row followed by the satellite during image acquisition. It, along with the Path, helps to specify the geographic location of the imagery.

UTM Zone

The UTM Zone specifies the specific zone of the Universal Transverse Mercator projection used for the imagery. It divides the Earth into longitudinal zones, each covering 6 degrees of longitude.

Output Format

The Output Format indicates the format in which the satellite imagery is delivered or exported. In this case, "GEOTIFF" refers to the GeoTIFF format, which is a common file format used for georeferenced raster images.

Collection Category

The Collection Category specifies the category or level of data collection. In this case, "T1" typically refers to Level-1 Terrain Corrected data, which has undergone geometric correction to remove distortions caused by terrain variations.

Datum

The Datum refers to the reference ellipsoid or mathematical model used to represent the shape of the Earth's surface for geospatial calculations and map projections. In this case, "WGS84" refers to the World Geodetic System 1984 datum, which is commonly used in global positioning systems (GPS) and satellite navigation systems.

Orientation

The Orientation specifies the orientation or alignment of the imagery. In this case, "NORTH_UP" indicates that the imagery is oriented with the north direction at the top of the image.
